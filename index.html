Nexus is a unique ocean world where chemosynthesis thrives. Unlike Earth's photosynthesis-based ecosystems, Nexus's life forms derive energy from chemical reactions in the absence of sunlight.

This alien environment hosts a diverse array of organisms adapted to extreme conditions, offering valuable insights into the potential for life in the cosmos.

Moons

of Nexus

Kreon (Big)

Kreon is the larger of Nexus's two moons. Its surface is covered in a thick layer of ice, beneath which lies a vast ocean. The tidal forces from Nexus create enough heat to maintain this liquid water, making Kreon a potential harbor for extraterrestrial life.

Distance from Nexus

Approximately 60,000 km

Diameter

3,200 km

Role

Kreon stabilizes the planet's axial tilt.

Impact on Life

Creates a steady environment, which helps aquatic species adapt.

Vara (Small)

Vara, the smaller moon, is a rocky body with a thin atmosphere. Its surface is marked by numerous impact craters and vast lava plains, evidence of past volcanic activity. Despite its small size, Vara plays a crucial role in Nexus's tidal systems.

Distance from Nexus

Approximately 25,000 km

Diameter

1,200 km

Role

Vara causes significant tidal movements due to its proximity.

Impact on Life

Generates diverse underwater habitats through fluctuating water levels.

6 Need-to-Know Facts About Nexus

ROCKY PLANET

The rocky exoplanet Nexus is rich in volcanic activity and deep-sea hydrothermal vents, creating a unique environment where chemosynthetic life thrives in diverse forms.

CHEMOSYNTHETIC LIFE

The Nexus ocean, a dark alien realm, hosts a thriving ecosystem driven by chemosynthesis, showcasing life's adaptability and potential for unconventional existence.

GEOLOGICAL ACTIVITY

Nexus's surface, a mix of dark rocky plains and bright icy deposits, resembles a celestial eye, revealing its complex and dynamic history.

GRAVITATIONAL PULL OF NEXUS

Nexus's larger radius and stronger gravity create a more intense gravitational pull than Earth's, affecting its atmosphere, weather, and geological activity.

FAR, FAR AWAY

Kreon's stabilizing influence and Vara's disruptive tidal forces on Nexus create a unique environment that fosters a diverse and dynamic ecosystem.

LIVING ON THE EDGE

Frequent solar flares from Nexus's red dwarf star expose life to harsh radiation, likely shaping its evolution in unique ways.
