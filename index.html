Nexus is a unique ocean world where chemosynthesis thrives. Unlike Earth's photosynthesis-based ecosystems, Nexus's life forms derive energy from chemical reactions in the absence of sunlight.

This alien environment hosts a diverse array of organisms adapted to extreme conditions, offering valuable insights into the potential for life in the cosmos.

Moons

of Nexus

Kreon (Big)

Kreon is the larger of Nexus's two moons. Its surface is covered in a thick layer of ice, beneath which lies a vast ocean. The tidal forces from Nexus create enough heat to maintain this liquid water, making Kreon a potential harbor for extraterrestrial life.

Distance from Nexus

Approximately 60,000 km

Diameter

3,200 km

Role

Kreon stabilizes the planet's axial tilt.

Impact on Life

Creates a steady environment, which helps aquatic species adapt.

Vara (Small)

Vara, the smaller moon, is a rocky body with a thin atmosphere. Its surface is marked by numerous impact craters and vast lava plains, evidence of past volcanic activity. Despite its small size, Vara plays a crucial role in Nexus's tidal systems.

Distance from Nexus

Approximately 25,000 km

Diameter

1,200 km

Role

Vara causes significant tidal movements due to its proximity.

Impact on Life

Generates diverse underwater habitats through fluctuating water levels.
